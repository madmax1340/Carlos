Middle Age Instruments

In the Renaissance, instruments were played by professionals for entertainment. These instruments were tools used to create sounds that could be put together and played rhythmically. Instruments can make music that may appeal to different human emotions. They were made by many people across Europe. They formed the basic concepts behind popular instruments today. Most instruments today were made or have had a main influence from instruments from the Renaissance. Without many instruments from the Renaissance, we would lose many great tools to create music and sounds. Many instruments are also used in great business practices and even brand logos today.


The Hurdy Gurdy

AKA: the wheel fiddle, the hurdy gurdy is a wodden instrument played by turning a madle with plays the notes. This instrument was very poplular among the French.

The Sackbut

The sackbut was a wind isntrument that lead the development of the trombone. It was also popular in France, and was likely made in the region of Burgundy.

The Irish Harp

The Irish Harp was the most popular instrument in Ireland and Scotland in the Middle Ages. The harp is even the logo of the Guinness brand today. It is also the national symbol of Ireland.
